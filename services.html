Why Recycle?

Resources

Disposing of old electricals in landfills or through illegal dumping, rather than recycling them, results in the permanent loss of valuable resources. This poses a significant environmental and economic challenge as these materials are non-renewable. Addressing this issue is crucial for sustainability and the conservation of essential resources.

economy

Each year, India's economy suffers a significant loss of 370 million due to the mismanagement of discarded or hoarded household electricals. These items contain valuable raw materials like gold, copper, aluminum, and steel, which, when discarded improperly or left unused, are wasted resources that could otherwise be reclaimed through recycling. This economic drain emphasizes the urgent need for improved recycling and resource recovery practices to not only mitigate financial losses but also promote sustainable and environmentally responsible habits, ensuring that these precious materials are not squandered and can be put to productive use for the nation's benefit.

Climate Change

The production of electricals generates substantial carbon emissions, contributing to environmental concerns. However, recycling old electricals can play a pivotal role in mitigating this issue. It has the potential to reduce carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions to an extent equivalent to removing approximately 1.3 million cars from the road. This impact arises from the conservation of resources, energy, and materials involved in manufacturing new electricals. Therefore, promoting recycling practices not only minimizes the burden of e-waste but also significantly contributes to the fight against climate change by lowering the carbon footprint associated with the production and disposal of electrical devices.
