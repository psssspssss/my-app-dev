How electrical recycling works?

Our unwanted electricals contain valuable materials including plastis and metals such as gold, copper, steel and aluminium. From you recycling point old electroniccs go to a reprocessing plant, where people sort and break them apart by hand. Then machines shred the materials. Magnets seperate steel and other ferrous metal and electrical currents remove other metals. Plastic is also sorted into different types and group up. Recovered metals and plastics become valuable commodities to the economy. They're sold on the open market and can become new products - from bicycles to life saving equipment.

Benefits of recycling old electricals?

More sustainable

Recycling equils a cleaner environment. If we recycled all our old electricals we would cut as much CO2 as taking 1.3 million cars off the road.

More from less

Each year, more than 7500 crores of precious metals could be salvaged from our old ectricals - including enough gold to make more than 850,000 rings.

More jobs

Recycling our old electricals would create hundreads of new jobs in electrical reuse and recycling - and save valuable raw materials like gold, copper and steel.
